--- ## **1. Summarizing (short)** Anne gives simple Hanukkah gifts to her family. She gives **capsules** to Mr. Dussel, **shampoo** to Mrs. Van Daan, and **cigarettes** to Mr. Van Daan. She also gives her father a **knitted** scarf. Her gifts show care and hope even in hard times. --- ## **2. Cause and Effect** **Cause: Mouschi** **Effect:** Mr. Van Daan gets angry and wants to get rid of the cat, which causes an argument with Peter. **Cause: the lampshade crash** **Effect:** Everyone thinks they have been discovered and becomes very frightened. **Cause: the thief** **Effect:** The family argues because they fear the thief may report them to the Green Police. --- ## **3. Important Quotation** **What is Mr. Van Daan referring to?** He is referring to Peter causing noise and trouble, especially the lamp crash. **How do these words make Peter feel?** They make Peter feel hurt, angry, and embarrassed. **What do these words reveal about Mr. Van Daan?** They show that Mr. Van Daan is harsh, angry, and unfair toward Peter. --- ## **4. Character’s Point of View (Mr. Frank)** At the end of Act I, Mr. Frank believes they must keep faith and courage. He thinks fear should not control them and that hope helps them survive. --- ## **5. Theme / Central Idea** **✔ fear** Fear is an important theme in Act I. The families live in constant fear of being discovered. When the lampshade crashes and a thief enters, everyone panics and argues. Anne faints because she is overwhelmed by fear. Even during Hanukkah, their fear shows how dangerous their situation is. ---
