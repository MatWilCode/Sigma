Katherine Johnson was a pivotal mathematician who worked at NASA during the Space Race. She helped calculate flight trajectories and launch windows for spacecraft. Her work was truly extraordinary, as it enabled astronauts to travel safely and smoothly into space and return to Earth. For instance, her calculations helped Alan Shepard fly into space and return safely. Without her mathematical expertise, those missions would have been far more perilous and less successful in various respects. Katherine Johnson also contributed to missions bound for the Moon. Her calculations were utilized in the mission that successfully landed Neil Armstrong on the lunar surface. Although she faced racism, she continued to work diligently and prove her capabilities. Her contributions helped NASA achieve success in the Space Race and demonstrated that women—particularly Black women—played a monumental role in the fields of science and space exploration.
