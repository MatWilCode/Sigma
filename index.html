Welcome to the Autumn Harvest Calculator! In this activity, you’ll create a Python program to help farmers calculate their earnings from selling pumpkins, apples, and corn. Your task is to write a program that does the following: Get User Inputs: Ask the user for the price per pumpkin, price per apple, and price per ear of corn. Ask the user how many pumpkins, apples, and ears of corn they plan to sell. Calculate the Earnings: Calculate the total earnings for each item by multiplying the price by the quantity. Calculate the overall total earnings by adding up the earnings from pumpkins, apples, and corn. Display the Results: Print out the earnings for each item (pumpkins, apples, corn). Print out the overall total earnings. For example: Price per pumpkin: 5 Price per apple: 1 Price per ear of corn: 0.5 Number of pumpkins: 10 Number of apples: 50 Number of ears of corn: 100 The output should look something like: Pumpkin earnings: 50 Apple earnings: 50 Corn earnings: 50.0 Total earnings: 150.0
